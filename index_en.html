Welcome !

By good will or good fortune, you have arrived on my online portfolio. Here, you'll be able to find my résumé, my contact information as well as a description and the source code of some of my work.

So, Who am I ?

My name is Tristan Roche. After a Diploma of higher education in information technology, I obtained a Master’s Degree in Cognitive science (diplôme d'ingénieur en cognitique) from l’École Nationale Supérieure de Cognitique in Bordeaux, France. I am currently looking for work.

During my learning course there, on top of improving my knowledge of programming, I got the chance to be introduced to Artificial Intelligence by creating, for instance, machine learning algorithms (supervised or not) able to discriminate points into categories, path-finding algorithmes finding the shortest path in a grid with constraints or even by creating multi-agent systems.

I also had the opportunity to learn about User eXperience issues such as the analysis of an action's cognitive load in order to minimize it, User interfaces' ergonomics, or even helping the user find their way through an application.

Enough with the education talk, who is the man behind all that ?

As you could see on my identity card, my name is Tristan Roche, but you might have met me online under the nickname StanFear.

I'm 23 years old, and I live in France, or to be more precise, in the south of la Drôme.

My main Passion ? programming !

"Most good programmers do programming not because they expect to get paid or get adulation by the public, but because it is fun to program." Linus Torvalds

The reason I'm quoting Linus Torvalds is not because I don't like being paid, or being thanked by the people using what I made, it is simply because, as he put it, it is fun to program !

What I am particularly interested about programming is AI development, the way they can be quite simple and yet take coherent decisions in a split second, or even, how multi-agents systems can have very simple entities rules and yet show a very interesting group behavior.

To stay on the same topic, I am also a videogames enthousiast (shocking isn't it ?) : Roller Coaster Tycoon, Railroad tycoon 2, Minecraft, and the one I am currently the most invested in : Factorio, a game in which the player, after crashing on an unknown planet, needs to automate the production of ressources such as iron plates, copper plates, circuits, munition to defend against the natives... The final goal being to launch a rocket in space.
